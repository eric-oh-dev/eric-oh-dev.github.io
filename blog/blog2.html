Identity, Values, and Strengths Exploration

Who am I and what I bring to the table

10 November 2010

Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

When I was younger, there was a situation where a group of friends pressured me to steal candies from a supermarket, I was faced with a difficult ethical decision. I weighed values like honesty, respect for others, personal integrity, and the pressure to fit in with my friends. Choosing not to participate meant prioritizing my principles over peer pressure, which felt uncomfortable but ultimately aligned with my values. Reflecting on this decision now, I feel proud of standing firm in my integrity, as it strengthened my ability to resist peer pressure and trust my own judgment. The experience taught me the importance of making ethical decisions, even when it’s challenging, and how to navigate situations where values are tested.

Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

My journey of moving from Korea to New Zealand at a young age brought together different cultural influences that shaped my values and identity. Growing up in Korea, I have been raised with strong values such as respect for family, community, and tradition, which are central to Korean culture. These values often emphasize hard work, loyalty, and honoring one’s elders, all of which have been instilled in me from an early age. When I moved to New Zealand, the culture shift introduced new perspectives on individuality, diversity, and openness. The Kiwi culture encouraged a more laid-back, egalitarian approach to life, where people are generally friendly, open-minded, and value personal freedom. Over time, these cultural elements influenced how I balance the traditional values from my Korean upbringing with the more relaxed, inclusive attitudes I encountered in New Zealand. The blending of both cultures helped me create a unique identity that honors my roots while embracing the diversity and opportunities that New Zealand offers.

Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

Having moved from Korea to New Zealand at a young age, I’ve developed the ability to adapt to new environments and cultures. This ability will help me navigate different learning styles, tackle new challenges, and embrace diverse perspectives as I continue my education. Also rooted in Korean culture, the value of hard work and persistence is often emphasized. This work ethic will be a powerful tool in pushing through difficult tasks, staying focused, and consistently striving for improvement, even when faced with challenges.

Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

With everything I've discussed so far, I tend to strive for perfection and sometimes this could be my limitation. It's often the case that I would procrastinate. I'm afraid of producing imperfect work and delay starting a task, thinking that I need everything to be just right before I begin. In other cases I would spend too much time on small details, neglecting the bigger picture. I find myself working excessively on tasks, which can lead to exhaustion and stress.

Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

When I was in University working on a group project, I had dealt with a group member who promises to contribute but doesn't follow through. This situation created tension within the team and hindered the overall progress of the project. I've tried to clearly communicate the importance of each person contributing their fair share. Also directly asked the member about their contributions, checked in on their progress, and expressed how it affects the group as a whole. My final resource was having a group check-in but the member only showed up once and was never seen again. Finally the problem was resolved by kicking the member out of the group and getting an extension on our project. To be honest, I don't think trying other strategies would have been effective but maybe providing support and having frequent group check-in would've been more motivating for the team.
