Discussing my understanding of Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset and how it influences learning

15 April 2025

Neuroplasticity & Growth Mindset: The Dream Team Behind Learning

Ever felt like your brain just isn’t wired for something? Maybe you hit a wall with a tricky coding challenge or struggled to understand a new concept. The good news? Your brain is way more flexible than you think—and your mindset plays a huge part in unlocking its potential.
Let’s talk about two powerful forces that can transform the way you learn: Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset.

What is Neuroplasticity?

Neuroplasticity is the brain’s ability to rewire itself by forming new neural connections. In simple terms: your brain can change.
Every time you practice something new—like learning JavaScript, solving bugs, or even typing faster—your brain is strengthening those connections. And the more you repeat the task, the stronger those pathways become. Think of it like building a mental muscle.
Why it matters:
- You’re not born “bad at math” or “not a tech person.” Those are just stories.
- Practice literally changes your brain.
- Struggling isn’t a sign of failure—it’s a sign that your brain is growing.

Growth Mindset: Fuel Your Brain

While neuroplasticity is about what your brain can do, Growth Mindset is about what you believe it can do.
Coined by psychologist Dr. Carol Dweck, Growth Mindset is the belief that your abilities can improve with effort, learning, and persistence. On the flip side is Fixed Mindset—the idea that your intelligence and skills are set in stone.
Why Growth Mindest rocks:
- It helps you keep going when things get tough.
- It makes failure feel like feedback, not defeat.
- It encourages you to focus on progress, not perfection.

Combine The Two = Superpowered Learning

When you combine neuroplasticity with a growth mindset, magic happens. You begin to see challenges not as roadblocks, but as opportunities. You start to realize that every bug you fix, every concept you grasp, every hour you code—you’re building a better, smarter brain.
This is the mindset shift that turns beginners into developers.

Real Talk: Applying This in a Bootcamp

If you’re currently in a coding bootcamp (like I am), these ideas aren’t just nice to know—they’re essential survival tools. Here’s how they’ve helped me so far:
- When I hit a wall, I remind myself: “My brain is rewiring itself right now.”
- I try to celebrate the effort, not just the result.
- I treat debugging as a puzzle, not a punishment.
- I journal the challenges I faced each day and what I learned—kind of like a “Growth Mindset Diary.”

Final Thoughts

You don’t have to be a genius to learn something new. You just need the right mindset and the patience to grow. Your brain is built for learning—and you’re more capable than you think.
“It’s not that I’m so smart, it’s just that I stay with problems longer.” — Albert Einstein
So, next time you feel stuck or frustrated, take a breath and smile. That’s just your brain doing its thing—growing, adapting, and becoming better.
