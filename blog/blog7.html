My Wellbeing Plan - Te Whare Tapa Wha

Learning something as big as web development can feel like a rollercoaster. To stay grounded and grow well, I’m using Te Whare Tapa Whā—a Māori model of wellbeing that helps me check in with all parts of who I am: body, mind, spirit, and relationships. When all four walls of my whare (house) are strong, I feel strong too.

Taha Tinana -0 Physical Wellbeing

How my body grows, feels, and moves.
To support my taha tinana, I will:
- Start each day with some light stretching or a short walk to wate my body up.
- Take regular breaks while studying to move my body and avoid sitting for too long.
- Prepare simple, healthy meals that give me consistent energy throughout the day.
- Keep a good sleep routine - even if I'm up late debugging! Rested body = sharper mind.
Why it matters: When my body feels good , it's so much easier to stay focused , deal with stress, and enjoy learning.

Taha Hinengaro - Mental & Emotional Wellbeing

My thoughts, emotions, and how I handle them.
To support my taha hinengaro, I will:
- Practice self-compassion: remind myself it's okay to make mistakes - it's part of learning
- Journal daily to reflect on challenges, wins, and how I'm feeling.
- Use mindfulness or breathing techniques when I feel overwhelmed.
- Stay aware of what I eat and how it affects my mood and focus.
Why it matters: Learning to code can be mentally tough - so staying emotionally grounded helps me stay motivated and not spiral when things get tricky.

Taha Wairua - Spiritual Wellbeing

My sense of meaning, purpose, and connection to something greater.
To support my taha wairua, I will:
- Reflect on why I chose this path - what drives me and what kind of developer I want to be.
- Spend quite time outdoors, even just sitting under a tree or listening to birds.
- Practice gratitude - writing down 3 things I'm thankful for each week.
- Honour my own values and let them guide how I interact with others and my work.
Why it matters: When I feel connected to something bigger than myself - my purpose, nature, the universe - I feel more at peace and grounded.

Taha Whanau - Family & Social Wellbeing

The people I care about and who care about me.
To support my taha whanau, I will:
- Make time for calls or catch-ups with friends and whanau, even during busy weeks.
- Share my learning journey with people I trust - let them encourage me.
- Be open to forming new connections with my cohort and see them as part of my learning whanau.
- Offer help to others when I can - it strengthens both them and me.
Why it matters: I'm not in this alone. Being supported and staying connected gives me strength and reminds me who I'm doing this for.

Whenua - Land & Connection to Nature

The foundation that grounds all the walls of my whare.
To strengthen my connection to whenua, I will:
- Take study breaks outside - even just 5 mintues in the sun or fresh air.
- Notice and appreciate the nature around me: birds, flowers, the wind, the sky.
- Visit local parks, beaches, or maunga when I need to rest.
- Acknowledge the whenua I'm learning on, and reflect on my place in Aotearoa.
Why it matters: Spending time in nature keeps me grounded. It reminds me I'm part of something bigger, and that there's calm even in chaos.

Final Thoughts

Te Whare Tapa Wha reminds me that I'm more than just a student learning code - I'm a whole person. When I care for my body, my mind, my spirit, my relationships, and my connection to the land, I give myself the best chance to thrive - at Dev Academy and beyond.
